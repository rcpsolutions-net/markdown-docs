# Consultant Spend Report: Structure & Required Figures

A **consultant spend report** tracks, audits, and optimizes expenditures on external firms and contractors. Visually, it is structured into three main layers:
* **Executive Dashboard:** High-level metrics showing total spend, active vendors, and budget variances.
* **Visual Charts:** Breakdown graphs tracking spend by **Category** (e.g., IT, Strategy), **Business Unit**, and **Supplier Concentration**.
* **The Spend Matrix:** A granular, sortable data table for row-by-row project auditing.

---

## Required Figures & Data Points

To build the report, compile your data into four distinct pillars:

### 1. Financials & Rates
* **Actual Spend vs. Budget:** Total invoiced amount to date compared against the authorized Statement of Work (SOW) budget.
* **Fee Structure:** Classification of payment types (Fixed Fee, Time & Materials, or Retainer).
* **Rate Benchmarking:** Blended hourly or daily rates categorized by resource level (e.g., Partner vs. Analyst).
* **Expenses:** Separate tracking for Travel & Expense (T&E) and administrative markups.

### 2. Vendor Metrics
* **Supplier Tier:** Categorization by firm type (e.g., Big Four, Boutique Specialists, Independent Contractors).
* **Tail Spend:** Identification of low-value, unmanaged, or off-contract consulting purchases.

### 3. Internal Alignment
* **Cost Center / Sponsor:** The department and executive owning the budget.
* **Project Taxonomy:** The specific capability being purchased (e.g., Digital Transformation, HR, Operations).

### 4. Compliance & Performance
* **Contract Compliance:** Percentage of spend routing through existing Master Service Agreements (MSAs).
* **Milestone Tracking:** Project delivery status against scheduled deadlines.
